# Small Business Lending Under the Equal Credit Opportunity Act (Regulation B)

## Document Information

- **Agency:** CFPB
- **Type:** Final Rule
- **Regulation:** 12 CFR Part 1002
- **Published:** 2026-05-01
- **Effective:** None

## Executive Summary

The CFPB is revising Regulation B, subpart B to amend coverage of credit transactions and financial institutions, redefine small business criteria, modify data collection requirements, and adjust compliance dates for Section 1071 small business lending data reporting.

## Analysis Summary

- **Obligations:** 15
- **Prohibitions:** 1
- **Deadlines:** 14
- **Agencies Mentioned:** 9

## Requirements

### Section 1

#### Obligations

1. **The CFPB must create rules and guidance to implement section 1071 requirements for small business lending data collection and enforcement.**
   - Who: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
   - Severity: HIGH

2. **Banks and other financial institutions are required to gather and submit information about their small business lending activities.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

#### Key Dates

- **2023-05-31:** Final rule implementing section 1071 by adding subpart B to Regulation B published
- **2024-07-03:** Interim final rule extending compliance dates published in Federal Register
- **2025-06-18:** Interim final rule published in Federal Register

### Section 2

#### Obligations

1. **Financial institutions must gather and submit specific data about credit applications from small businesses and women/minority-owned businesses to the CFPB.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

2. **Covered financial institutions must improve data quality to support the purposes of Section 1071 implementation.**
   - Who: covered financial institutions
   - Severity: MEDIUM

#### Key Dates

- **2026-06-30:** Final rule becomes effective
- **2028-01-01:** Compliance date for the rule
- **2022-01-06:** Comment period closed for 2021 proposed rule
- **2025-10-02:** Final rule published in Federal Register (2025 compliance date final rule)

### Section 3

#### Obligations

1. **Financial institutions must support efforts to enforce fair lending laws.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

2. **Institutions must provide data and information to help identify lending needs and opportunities for women-owned, minority-owned, and small businesses.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

3. **The CFPB accepted public comments on proposed amendments to small business lending rules.**
   - Who: public commenters
   - Severity: MEDIUM
   - Deadline: 2025-12-15

4. **The CFPB intends to implement small business lending data collection as a phased, long-term initiative similar to HMDA.**
   - Who: all financial institutions subject to section 1071
   - Severity: HIGH

5. **The CFPB has authority to modify data collection requirements to start with a narrower scope to ensure data quality.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

#### Key Dates

- **2025-12-15:** Submit comments on 2025 proposed rule amendments to small business lending data collection
- **2025-11-13:** CFPB published proposed rule to amend 2023 final rule in Federal Register
- **2025-05-06:** Court case CV–24882–DSL (S.D. Fla.) pending
- **2025-02-07:** 5th Circuit issued unpublished order tolling compliance deadlines in Texas Bankers Ass'n v. CFPB
- **2025-03-11:** E.D. Ky. issued order in Monticello Banking Co. v. CFPB case

### Section 4

#### Obligations

1. **The CFPB will implement section 1071 data collection gradually, beginning with essential lending products, major lenders, and key data points.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

2. **Financial institutions must implement the incremental approach to comply with section 1071 while minimizing disruption to small business lending markets.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

3. **The CFPB coordinated with prudential regulators and other agencies to ensure consistency with their objectives.**
   - Who: Federal agencies and prudential regulators
   - Severity: MEDIUM

#### Key Dates

- **2025-01-20:** Comply with Executive Order 14168 regarding removal of gender identity references and use of binary sex categories

### Section 5

#### Obligations

1. **Financial institutions must gather and submit specific information about small business loan applications to the CFPB.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: HIGH

2. **The CFPB has the legal authority to create regulations for small business lending data collection under section 1071.**
   - Who: all financial institutions subject to section 1071
   - Severity: CRITICAL

3. **The CFPB must create rules that require collection and reporting of small business lending information.**
   - Who: all financial institutions
   - Severity: CRITICAL

#### Key Dates

- **None:** compliance with final rule on small business lending data collection
